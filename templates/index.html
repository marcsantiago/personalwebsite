I graduated from Susquehanna University on May 12 of 2013. I majored in Biology and Philosophy and graduated with honors in biological research. During my time at Susquehanna University I worked as Dr. Jack Holt's research assistant. I completed my senior research with Dr. Jack Holt on May 2013. The primary focus of the research was to describe the Susquehanna River's upper main stem located near Byers Island. My research focused on the types of algal communities present in the Susquehanna River. More specifically, I worked with phytoplankton and diatom-periphyton to access water quality. Taxanomic groups were classified to species level via the use of a light microscrope and scanning electron microscope. My senior research focused on the differences and utility of using passive and active collection methods as suggested by the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA). The results of my research have been posted in the research section of this page. For more information regarding systemantics within the three domains of life please click here.
